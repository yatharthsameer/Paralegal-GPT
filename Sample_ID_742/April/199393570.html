Hon'ble Samit Gopal,J.

1. List revised.

2. Heard Sri Anuj Srivastava, learned counsel for the applicant, Sri Rakesh Kumar Srivastava, learned counsel for the first informant and Sri Jitendra Singh, learned brief holder for the State and perused the material on records.

3. This matter is nominated to this Bench vide order dated 27.02.2024 by Hon'ble The Chief Justice.

4. This bail application under Section 439 of Code of Criminal Procedure has been filed by the applicant- Vijay Khari, seeking enlargement on bail during trial in connection with Case Crime No. 241 of 2022, under Sections 498-A, 323, 504, 341, 325 and 304 I.P.C. and 3/4 D.P. Act, Police Station Link Road, District Ghaziabad.

5. The first information report of the present case was lodged on 09.07.2022, under Sections 323, 504, 341, 325, 498-A and 3/4 D.P. Act by Anjali against the applicant and 06 other persons namely Dharmwati (mother-in-law), Rajendra Khari (father-in-law), Kavita (nand), Babita (nand), Devendra Khari (Jeth), Vijay Khari (husband) and Rohit friend of husband with the allegation that she was married to Vijay Khari around three years back in which her parents had given money for car but still the accused persons used to torture her for dowry. She did not make any complaint for two years but now since the last one year her mother-in-law, father-in-law, nand and jeth taunt her daily and assault her. Her husband Vijay Khari and his friend Rohit yesterday with the other accused persons abused her and assaulted her due to which she received injury and then police was called on Dial 112 number. She told her father Ram Kishan about the incident and told him that the dispute with regards to dowry is going on since long and he should come immediately on which her father, tau, chacha and her brothers tried to settle the issue but the accused persons did not listen to them and locked them in the house with her. They being armed with hockey, lathi and danda resorted to assault her. Her jeth is a persons of criminal nature. Her husband and his friend Rohit were armed with sharp edged weapons. They assaulted her brother, father, tau, chacha due to which they received injuries and suffered facture and became unconscious. They were taken to the hospital with the help of police where their condition is serious. A report be lodged and action be taken.

6. Learned counsel for the applicant argued that although the applicant is the husband of the informant but he has been falsely implicated in the present case. It is argued that the version of the first information report is a concocted version in as much as the police after investigation has submitted a final report in favour of Kavita (nand), Babita (nand) and Rohit friend of the applicant. It is submitted that in the present matter four persons including the informant namely Smt. Anjali, Ram Kishan father of the informant, Ankit & Ashish brothers of the informant received injuries whereas Rameshwar the uncle of the informant is the deceased. It is submitted that after the death of Rameshwar section 304 I.P.C. was added in the matter. It is submitted that the deceased Rameshwar was found to have received a single injury which as per the postmortem report was described as hematoma on the inner aspect of left thigh with facture on left femur. The cause of death was opined as septicemic shock due to the mentioned injury. It is submitted that the present case has a cross version also of which a first information report was lodged as Case Crime No.242 of 2022, under Sections 147, 323, 504, 452, 506 I.P.C. against the family members of the informant in which the applicant along with Devendra Khari (brother of the applicant), Rajendra Singh (father of the applicant) and Smt. Dharamwati (mother of the applicant) received injuries and were medically examined. It is submitted that the said matter was investigated and a charge-sheet was submitted against the accused persons namely Ram Kishan, Jagbeer, Ankit, Siddharth and Ashish whereas Sunita (mother-in-law of the informant), Smt. Shashi, Smt. Arti, Ramu, son & wife of Ramu, Omveer and 10-12 unknown persons were exonerated. It is submitted that the factum regarding the injuries as received from the side of the applicant have not been disclosed by the prosecution in the present matter and as such there is suppression of relevant material. It is further argued that general and omnibus allegations have been levelled against the applicant and all the accused persons. The author of the injury to the deceased is not known. The applicant has no criminal history as stated in para 20 of the affidavit and is in jail since 16.07.2022.

7. Per contra, learned counsel for the first informant vehemently opposed the prayer for bail and submitted that that bail of co-accused Devendra Khari has been rejected by a co-ordinate Bench of this Court vide order dated 19.11.2022 passed in Criminal Misc. Bail Application No. 52070 of 2022 (Devendra Khari vs. State of U.P.), the copy of the order has been produced before the Court which is taken on record. It is submitted that co-accused who was the jeth of the informant was denied bail by another Bench of this Court and the applicant is the husband of the informant who is named in the first information report. It is submitted that looking to the facts and the active involvement of the applicant, the bail application be rejected.

8. Learned counsel for the State has also adopted the argument of learned counsel for the first informant.

9. After having heard learned counsel for the parties and perusing the record, it is evident that although co-accused Devendra Khari has been denied bail by another Bench of this Court vide order dated 19.11.2022 but reading of the said order goes to show that the Court in the order states that none of the members of the applicant side sustained any injury whereas the fact of the matter which is not disputed is that three persons from the side of the applicant have received injuries and were medically examined. General and omnibus allegations have been levelled against the applicant and all the accused persons. The author of the injury of the deceased is not known. There is a cross version of the incident in which also charge-sheet has been submitted. The fact as to who is the aggressor cannot be decided at this stage.

10. Looking to the facts and circumstances of this case, the nature of evidence and also the absence of any convincing material to indicate the possibility of tampering with the evidence, this Court is of the view that the applicant may be enlarged on bail.

11. Let the applicant- Vijay Khari, be released on bail in the aforesaid case crime number on furnishing a personal bond and two sureties each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the court concerned with the following conditions which are being imposed in the interest of justice:-

i) The applicant will not tamper with prosecution evidence and will not harm or harass the victim/complainant in any manner whatsoever.
ii) The applicant will abide the orders of court, will attend the court on every date and will not delay the disposal of trial in any manner whatsoever.
(iii) The applicant shall file an undertaking to the effect that he shall not seek any adjournment on the date fixed for evidence when the witnesses are present in court. In case of default of this condition, it shall be open for the trial court to treat it as abuse of liberty of bail and pass orders in accordance with law.
(iv) The applicant will not misuse the liberty of bail in any manner whatsoever. In case, the applicant misuses the liberty of bail during trial and in order to secure his presence proclamation under section 82 Cr.P.C., may be issued and if applicant fails to appear before the court on the date fixed in such proclamation, then, the trial court shall initiate proceedings against him, in accordance with law, under section 174-A I.P.C.
(v) The applicant shall remain present, in person, before the trial court on dates fixed for (1) opening of the case, (2) framing of charge and (3) recording of statement under Section 313 Cr.P.C. If in the opinion of the trial court absence of the applicant is deliberate or without sufficient cause, then it shall be open for the trial court to treat such default as abuse of liberty of bail and proceed against him in accordance with law and the trial court may proceed against him under Section 229-A IPC.
(vi) The trial court may make all possible efforts/endeavour and try to conclude the trial expeditiously after the release of the applicant.

12. The identity, status and residential proof of sureties will be verified by court concerned and in case of breach of any of the conditions mentioned above, court concerned will be at liberty to cancel the bail and send the applicant to prison.

13. The bail application is allowed.

14. Pending application (s), if any, shall stand disposed of.
